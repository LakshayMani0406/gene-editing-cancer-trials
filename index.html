Clinical-trial analytics · ClinicalTrials.gov

A trial-success model that looked 90% right. Then I removed the cheating.

An observational analysis of ~4,460 oncology gene-editing and cell-therapy trials. I trained a model to predict whether a registered trial would complete. The first version scored AUC 0.90. I did not trust it, so I took it apart, one layer of leakage at a time, until what was left was the honest answer: prospective completion is close to a coin flip.

0.90
Leaked: full features, including conduct-time signals
0.72
Naive-clean: registration-only, but the label still cheats
0.60
Strict + temporal: the honest, prospective answer

Same random forest at every step. Only the features, the label, and the train/test split change.
